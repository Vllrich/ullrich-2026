## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Not immediately "AI made this." The ASCII fluid simulation hero is technically impressive and on-brand. The voice is earned and direct. However, the restrained editorial treatment (near-white body, single cobalt accent, horizontal rules everywhere, tracked uppercase labels on every section) lands close to the saturated 2026 editorial-typographic lane the brand register warns against. Single Darker Grotesque across all roles flattens typographic texture. The clearest AI tell: uppercase tracked labels appear on the hero kicker, disciplines section, clients strip, service category tags, service format tags, footer nav, and nav links - the same grammar repeated seven or eight times.

**Deterministic scan**: 1 finding - `single-font` warning in `src/layouts/Layout.astro`. Darker Grotesque is assigned to both `--font-display` and `--font-sans`. The detector did not catch the uppercase-label repetition or contrast failures; those are LLM-only findings.

## Overall Impression

The bones are strong - the copy is direct and non-generic, the ASCII canvas hero is genuinely distinctive, and the section structure is clean. What's holding it back: the typographic system is doing too little (single font, near-invisible rule-color text in two places, every label tracked uppercase), mobile navigation is broken at the smallest breakpoint, and every section after the hero is pure text - no imagery, no proof of the twenty-year creative past. The single biggest opportunity: one real artifact of that biography - a photograph, a project still - to ground the claimed history in visible reality.

## What's Working

**1. The hero canvas.** WebGL fluid simulation rendered as ASCII art is technically ambitious, visually distinctive, and directly related to the background in real-time graphics. It's the one element a visitor will not have seen on a comparable site.

**2. The copy and voice.** "Tell me what you're building and where it's stuck. If I'm the right person to help, I'll say so - and if I'm not, I'll tell you that too." Earned confidence, not performance. The Perspective section's four-step method reads as someone who has done it.

**3. Accessibility fundamentals.** `focus-visible` with cobalt outline is correct. Fade animations start visible by default, transition only with `.js-ready`. `prefers-reduced-motion` is handled for scroll reveals and client marquee. Form fields have explicit `label[for]` pairs and inline error spans.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Mobile navigation disappears entirely below 500px

`@media (max-width: 500px) { .nav-links { display: none; } }` with no hamburger or alternative. A mobile visitor cannot navigate to Services or Contact without scrolling through all seven sections. Primary conversion action ("Start a conversation") is reachable only by full-page scroll. Fix: minimal hamburger toggle at ≤500px. Suggested command: `/impeccable adapt`

### [P1] Rule-color text is functionally invisible in two places

`.meta-coords` in `About.astro` uses `var(--rule, oklch(87% 0.005 260))` on white (~1.2:1 contrast). `.method-statement p` in `Perspective.astro` uses the same color on `--surface` background (~1.1:1). WCAG AA requires 3:1 minimum for large text. Fix: use `--ghost` (L=50%) for coordinates; use `--dim` (L=28%) for the method statement's introductory line. Suggested command: `/impeccable audit`

### [P1] No imagery in six of seven sections

After the hero canvas, every section is pure text on white/surface. The biography claims twenty years across opera houses, GPU pipelines, and galleries - none of that is shown. Brand register reference is explicit: "Text-only pages where typography alone carries the entire visual weight are the failure mode." Fix: at minimum one real photograph in About; one project artifact anywhere to ground the narrative. Suggested command: `/impeccable shape`

### [P2] Single font family throughout

Both `--font-display` and `--font-sans` are Darker Grotesque. Every typographic role - display headings, body copy, labels, metadata - is the same family. Typographic texture is flat. Fix: pair with a different body font, or introduce a refined mono for metadata labels only. Suggested command: `/impeccable typeset`

### [P2] Uppercase tracked labels on every section - the AI scaffolding tell

Hero kicker, nav links, disciplines label, clients strip label, service category tags, service format tags, footer nav - eight instances of tracked uppercase as section grammar. The brand register bans this pattern unless it is a deliberate named system. Fix: keep instances that carry information the heading doesn't (service tags, client strip). Remove or redesign the decorative ones. Suggested command: `/impeccable distill`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: "RAG", "agents", "RAG & agents" appear without explanation across WhatIDo and Services. No pricing signal. No sense of what a conversation costs or commits to. Medium abandonment risk at Services section.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Nav gone at 500px. No persistent CTA as user scrolls seven sections. WebGL canvas may drain battery or delay render with no static fallback for the hero right panel.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)**: `<dl>` in About uses only `<dd>` without `<dt>` labels - screen readers announce values without context. WebGL canvas continues animating regardless of `prefers-reduced-motion`. Overall accessibility baseline is above average for this category; these are fixable.

## Minor Observations

1. `<dl>` in `About.astro` uses only `<dd>` elements without paired `<dt>`. Add `<dt>` labels (Location, Open to) or restructure as `<ul>`.
2. WebGL canvas has no `prefers-reduced-motion` check. Add `window.matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)').matches` at animation start to stop the frame loop for affected users.
3. Hero kicker uses "/" in "Fractional CTO/CAIO" while "·" is the separator elsewhere. Use "Fractional CTO · CAIO" for consistency.
4. No `<meta property="og:image">` - LinkedIn shares (the primary channel for this audience) will render no preview image.

## Questions to Consider

- "The site claims a twenty-year creative past in one sentence. What would it look like to show one artifact of it - one image that a visitor could not have guessed from the copy?"
- "The typography is restrained. Does that restraint signal precision to a technical founder, or does it signal caution? What would a confident version of this typography look like?"
- "Every section ends at the full 1200px max-width. What would happen if one section broke that constraint - full-bleed, unexpected proportion - just once?"
